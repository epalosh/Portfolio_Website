Research

I conducted climate science research in collaboration with other high school students through the NASA SEES program.

As a researcher, my responsibilites included:

  • Worked in a team to conduct research, analyze remote sensing satellite data, and present our findings in a symposium to NASA engineers and scientists.
  • Converted JavaScript documentation to Python for use in Google Colab; utilized MatPlotLib, Pandas, and Seaborn to access datasets, stratify data, and map it to analyze trends and contribute to our overall research purpose.
  • Trained machine learning models to recognize and label land cover types.
  • Published a web-app to display our findings in a more understandable format for the public.
  • Completed 20+ hours of prerequisite earth science modules, and participated in an additional Google Earth Engine (GEE) training prior to the internship.
  • Investigated trends in soil moisture, vegetation coverage, atmospheric moisture, ground surface temperatures, precipitation, and sea surface temperature in order to confirm a relationship between climates in Texas and Australia, and used a layered analysis to forecast at-risk areas for climate disasters such as flooding and wildfires.
  • Submitted our research paper abstract: “Visualizing Two Decades of Climate Disaster Impacts on Texas and Australia via Satellite Remote Sensing to Prepare for the 2022-2023 La Niña Phenomenon” to the AGU Earth Science Conference.
  • Learned from and networked with experienced mentors in the field.
  • Was selected among a pool of 1100 applicants (~8%).
